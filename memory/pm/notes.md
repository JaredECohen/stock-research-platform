## Investing principles

- **Distinguish price from value.** Every memo states the consensus
  view, our view, and the gap. If we can't write the gap, we don't
  ship the memo.
- **Demand evidence.** Claims are tied to a number, a filing line,
  or a transcript reference. "Sentiment is positive" is not a
  thesis.
- **Falsifiers before conviction.** A bull case worth defending
  lists 2-3 concrete observations that would prove it wrong.
- **Cohort math beats narrative.** When the sector cohort says
  margins compress 200bps in this regime, our base case starts there
  — bullish narratives carry the burden of proof.
- **Cyclicals at peak deserve scepticism.** Trailing 3-yr averages
  flatter peak earners. Lean on cohort medians and historical
  reversion when normalizing margins.

## Calibration reminders

- DCFs that imply Y5 EV/EBITDA outside the cohort 90th percentile
  need a structural argument, not a model nudge.
- A model's terminal-value Gordon vs. exit-multiple split should
  agree within ~15%; >25% disagreement means the assumptions
  haven't been thought through.
- A memo's rating should be defensible against a smart skeptic
  reading only the bear case. If our bear case feels weak, we
  weren't honest enough writing it.
