# Quality compounders: pre-purchase checklist

A name only earns the "core position" treatment after passing all six. Failing
even one drops it into the trading-position bucket where sizing is half and
exit discipline tightens.

1. **Sustainable ROIC > 15%.** Not LTM — five-year average through at least one
   downturn. Mean-reverting ROIC is a cycle play, not a compounder.
2. **Owner-operator or shareholder-aligned management.** Insider ownership,
   compensation tied to per-share economics, not stock-based-comp issuance for
   "alignment".
3. **Customer concentration < 25%** in the top customer. Above that, the moat
   is the customer's, not yours.
4. **Decade-plus addressable runway.** TAM that doesn't exhaust within the
   forward DCF window.
5. **FCF conversion > 80%.** Income that doesn't translate to cash means the
   accounting and the economics aren't the same animal.
6. **Downside scenario clear and survivable.** If the bear thesis materializes
   and you can't articulate the recovery path, position-sizing should reflect
   that ambiguity.
